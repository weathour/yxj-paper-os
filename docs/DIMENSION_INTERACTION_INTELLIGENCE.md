# Dimension Interaction Intelligence

D00-D19 form a stable diagnostic vocabulary for paper planning. They are not an ordered workflow or a universal critical set. The cognitive kernel synthesizes all available files, artifacts, owner statements, active conflicts, and requested writing scope before choosing an action.

## Interaction principles

- Route/objective changes can affect claims, structure, and handoff; declare only the affected D IDs/scopes in Dependency Recheck.
- Material and result records retain local locators and explicit status; planned/unavailable material cannot support an active claim.
- Active claims require evidence IDs, warrant, scope, directness, uncertainty/counterevidence, wording boundaries, and a non-conflicted resolution.
- Lens requirements are conditional. An active `venue-template` lens blocks only explicitly linked writing-design scopes. A missing, malformed, unsupported, or small corpus never becomes a global blocker.
- Template-corpus measurements remain material/design observations: they may inform D09/D15/D17/D18 and scoped D19, but they cannot become a D06 evidence anchor or increase D11 claim support.
- A single rich answer may update several dimensions in one turn; a metadata-only edit remains lightweight.
- `04_WRITING_DESIGN_PACK.md` mirrors the authoritative Writing Scopes registry and distinguishes ready, partial, blocked, and deferred scopes.

## Safety boundary

The validator checks headings, columns, enums, typed references, locators, declared dependencies, placeholders, and forbidden promises. The template analyzer checks supported source structure, hashes, arithmetic, missingness, and registered descriptive metrics. Neither surface infers a hidden semantic graph or judges academic truth, novelty, venue fit, statistical correctness, prose/visual quality, acceptance, or future model behavior.
