# Dimension Interaction Intelligence

D00-D19 are a stable diagnostic vocabulary, not ordered tasks or a runtime graph. The
agent synthesizes the requested scope, repository evidence, six-file state, active
lenses, template-design evidence, owner decisions, and changed dependencies before
choosing among the unordered actions `inspect`, `derive`, `project`, `ask`, and
`validate`.

## Authority and dependency interactions

- `00_DIMENSION_INDEX.md` is the only scope-readiness authority and records affected
  D IDs/scopes/records during a dependency recheck.
- `01_MATERIALS_INVENTORY.md` keeps scientific `M-*` and design-only `TPL-*` roles
  separate, including separate records for a dual-role publication.
- `02_CLAIM_EVIDENCE_BOUNDARY.md` alone controls scientific support, uncertainty,
  counterevidence, and wording ceilings.
- `03_WRITING_STRUCTURE.md` owns section/paragraph/payload design, selected important
  paragraphs and controlled frames, language, visuals/captions/accessibility, direct
  edges, template-rule applications, soft budgets, and scoped plans.
- `04_WRITING_DESIGN_PACK.md` never mirrors 03 or readiness. It projects exactly seven
  coverage rows, one of five template modes, blockers/prohibitions, counts, and
  authority pointers for each scope.

A changed `DEC-*`, `M-*`, `C-*`, `TPL-*`, `TRULE-*`, or detailed record stales only
its declared paragraph/frame/visual/rule/scope dependents. Unrelated writer-ready
scopes remain ready; there is no inferred transitive closure.

## Template interactions

The five scope modes are `semantic_primary`, `semantic_plus_quantitative`,
`quantitative_only`, `generic_fallback`, and `not_applicable`. Semantic dossier state
and analyzer state are separate. Semantic-primary can be ready without analyzer
artifacts; quantitative analysis preserves metric, partition, denominator,
missingness, comparability, and conclusion-strength boundaries. Every mode preserves
the design-only firewall.

Template sources may affect D09/D14/D15/D17/D18 and a scoped D19 handoff. They cannot
become D06 evidence, increase D11 claim support, prove novelty/quality/venue fit, or
turn metadata-only access into full-text semantic observation.

## Mechanical versus semantic boundaries

Validators and the dashboard can prove declared schema, headings, columns, enums,
cardinality, typed IDs, pointers, fingerprints, freshness, public/hidden projection
agreement, selective blocker propagation, and the absence of obvious paste-ready prose
patterns. Counts are **declared structural coverage**, not semantic completeness.

They cannot prove that the agent found every important paragraph, that a design is
academically insightful, that prose or visuals will be good, that statistics are
scientifically correct, or that a paper fits a venue or will be accepted. Manual and
behavior review remain necessary for those judgments.
