# Nature absorption governance — yxj-paper-os

This reference governs source-level absorption of non-figure Nature skills into
yxj-paper-os. It is additive to the department I/O contract and does not expose
new public commands.

## Control rule

Nature skills are internal SOP capabilities only. They may guide production of
material candidates; they do not become public `$nature-*` surfaces, hidden
managers, paper-owner semantic authorities, final certifiers, or state-transition
authorities.

## M1 material chain

`NatureSourceInventory` records exactly what source implementation was absorbed.
`CompanySkillRegistry` maps each Nature capability to an internal yxj owner lane
with authority limits. `NatureAbsorptionPackage` is the root closure package that
links reader/search/citation/writing/polishing/data/reviewer/response/presentation
and patent-boundary materials plus backflow routes.

## Department placement

- Reader/search/citation/data availability: `evidence_and_method`.
- Writing section moves and journal style: `paper_architecture_and_narrative`.
- Polishing, presentation/PPT, and patent-draft candidates:
  `manuscript_and_figure_production`.
- Reviewer simulation and reviewer-response action mapping: `review_and_governance`.
- Registry/root absorption closure: `pmo`.

The presentation/PPT capability is part of writing/expression production. Export
may later package a deck, but export is not the primary owner of slide narrative,
speaker notes, or presentation rhetoric.

## Boundary gates

- Data identifiers, licences, access committees, embargoes, and accessions must
  be confirmed or source-located; do not invent them.
- Reviewer response must preserve editor/reviewer comment IDs and must not invent
  line numbers or manuscript changes.
- Patent drafts are drafting aids only; they require source support,
  `not_legal_opinion`, `no_patentability_guarantee`, and professional review.
- Any failed material routes to an explicit department backflow task and cannot
  close until validators pass and the result is ingested into ledgers.
