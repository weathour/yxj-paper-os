# D00-D19 Dimension Rubric — Sparse Cognitive Kernel

This rubric is a vocabulary and sufficiency aid. D00-D19 are diagnostic coverage pointers, not ordered tasks, a universal critical set, or a completion score. A dimension may be handled, deferred, absent, or blocked according to owner authority, available material, and the requested writing scope.

## Global distinctions

- `artifact-observed`: directly located workspace material; preserve a local locator.
- `owner-stated`: an owner-supplied decision or fact; do not silently promote it to evidence.
- `model-derived`: a grounded inference or projection; retain the input record IDs or file pointer.
- `model-proposed`: a candidate only; replace it with a new grounded substantive record if accepted.
- Support is a claim relation (`evidence-supported`, `evidence-partial`, `evidence-unsupported`, `not_applicable`), separate from origin and resolution.
- `writer-ready` is scoped to a requested writing surface and is weaker than research, manuscript, submission, publication, or semantic readiness.

## Dimension ladder

Each dimension uses these labels only as qualitative language: `minimum`, `standard`, and `ideal` describe confidence or usefulness, never a mandatory order or global gate. Record explicit blockers, owner-only decisions, stale dependencies, and safe derivations in the sparse tables when they affect a requested scope.

### D00 — Workspace metadata

**Current home:** `00_DIMENSION_INDEX.md`.

**Sufficiency:** slug, update marker, six-file schema version `0.2`, and dimension index identity are mechanically stable. Metadata-only edits remain lightweight.

### D01 — Owner decisions

**Current home:** route and decision records.

**Sufficiency:** consequential route, contribution, audience, or wording commitments are owner-stated or explicitly candidate/deferred. Never resolve an owner-only tradeoff by position in a checklist.

### D02 — Stale and dependency state

**Current home:** index Dependency Recheck.

**Sufficiency:** when a DEC/M/C record changes, declare affected D IDs/scopes and mark them current, rechecked, stale, candidate, blocked, or not_applicable. The validator checks the declaration, not an inferred runtime graph.

### D03 — Project brief

**Current home:** route file.

**Sufficiency:** problem, object, objective/RQ, assumptions, and important boundaries are grounded or explicitly absent/deferred.

### D04 — Target route profile

**Current home:** route file.

**Sufficiency:** venue family, paper/contribution context, audience, format constraints, and forbidden interpretations are owner-stated or clearly candidate. A known venue does not activate a global template requirement.

### D05 — Material inventory

**Current home:** materials file.

**Sufficiency:** record available artifacts, method/design facts, results, evidence items, sources, governance material, and explicit absences only when they have a real locator or stated boundary.

### D06 — Evidence inventory

**Current home:** materials and claim files.

**Sufficiency:** available/partial evidence has a resolvable material record and locator. Planned/unavailable material and planned visuals are not evidence for active claims.

### D07 — Source and citation bank

**Current home:** materials file.

**Sufficiency:** supplied or candidate sources have a role and boundary. Source notes do not establish source truth or claim support without a material/evidence relation.

### D08 — Research dossier

**Current home:** materials file.

**Sufficiency:** context, synthesis, conflict, counterevidence, and gap hypotheses remain bounded. A gap hypothesis is not a novelty judgment.

### D09 — Exemplar and language profile

**Current home:** structure file and optional `venue-template` lens.

**Sufficiency:** supplied exemplars or a current `venue-template` analysis may inform only linked surface constraints. If statistical analysis is active, preserve its `case_set`/`exploratory`/`distributional` mode, `documents`/`doc_id`/`partition` grounding, `.yxj-paper-os/template-analysis/corpus-summary.json` and `design-profile.json` pointers, missingness/limitations, rule type, and any owner-confirmed `deliberate_divergence`. Missing, malformed, stale, or PDF-only inputs affect only the linked template-design scope; they never globally block Methods, prove venue fit, or support D06/D11 scientific claims.

### D10 — Contribution options

**Current home:** claim and decision records.

**Sufficiency:** contribution forms and alternatives are recorded as owner decisions, candidates, or deferred options with material dependencies.

### D11 — Claim-evidence map

**Current home:** claim records.

**Sufficiency:** active/downgraded claims require evidence IDs, warrant, scope/conditions, directness, uncertainty/counterevidence, allowed/forbidden wording, support relation, and non-conflicted resolution.

### D12 — Wording boundary

**Current home:** claim and structure files.

**Sufficiency:** allowed and forbidden wording follows claim support and uncertainty. It constrains downstream drafting but does not draft prose here.

### D13 — Limitations and risks

**Current home:** claim/structure handoff.

**Sufficiency:** validity threats, uncertainty, mitigations, governance boundaries, and unresolved risks have a consequence and next action where relevant.

### D14 — Reader spine

**Current home:** structure file.

**Sufficiency:** reader questions and section responsibilities are model-derived from grounded inputs or explicitly owner-shaped; no fixed question sequence is required.

### D15 — Manuscript outline

**Current home:** structure file.

**Sufficiency:** section jobs, input record IDs, drafting boundaries, and route constraints are sufficient for the requested scope. This is a planning handoff, not manuscript prose.

### D16 — Object granularity

**Current home:** route, claims, and structure.

**Sufficiency:** the unit of contribution, claim, result, or section is explicit enough to prevent accidental scope drift; conflicts with owner authority require reconciliation.

### D17 — Surface control

**Current home:** claims and structure.

**Sufficiency:** surface-specific wording, terminology, audience, and visual constraints are bounded by the claim map and active lenses.

### D18 — Visual plan

**Current home:** materials and structure.

**Sufficiency:** existing/needed/deferred/absent visuals have roles, evidence boundaries, accessibility responsibilities, and downstream handoff notes. A planned visual cannot support an active claim.

### D19 — Scoped writing design pack

**Current home:** design pack.

**Sufficiency:** `Scoped Handoff` mirrors authoritative `Writing Scopes`, distinguishes ready from blocked/deferred scopes, carries traceable inputs and constraints, and makes no global or semantic readiness promise.

## Interaction guidance

Use whole-situation synthesis and composable lenses. Recheck only declared dependents after a changed record. Ask the owner when an unresolved choice changes commitment, audience, claim strength, or forbidden wording; otherwise inspect, record, derive, propose, reconcile, keep a claim inactive, or compile a scoped handoff without waiting for a form-completion sequence.
