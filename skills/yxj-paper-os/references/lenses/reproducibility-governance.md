# Reproducibility Governance

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate when methods, algorithms, experiments, data/code/material availability,
reporting checklists, supplements, ethics, or reproducibility statements affect a
requested scope. Record the exact method/reporting/statement surfaces.

## Reasoning model

Reproducibility design links each claim-bearing procedure to the artifacts and
conditions needed to understand, rerun, or delimit it. Distinguish scientific method
facts, implementation details, run configuration, availability state, reporting
obligation, and later statement wording. “Available,” “reproducible,” and “reported”
are not synonyms. Paragraph jobs should expose the minimal dependency chain before a
reader encounters results that rely on it.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use algorithms/formulas, parameter tables, code/data paths, environments, seeds,
calibration budgets, protocols, platform versions, licenses/permissions, availability
plans, reporting checklists, and explicit owner-gated release items. Derive method
paragraph jobs, artifact pointers, reporting/statement inventory, supplement handoff,
and prohibitions. Do not claim executability, availability, licensing, or compliance
without current evidence.

## Sufficiency predicates

- method/result claims point to required artifacts, parameters, bounds, and conditions;
- absent/deferred artifacts state the downstream claim and paragraph consequence;
- algorithm/equation/object responsibilities and reproducibility handoffs are placed;
- required statement categories are inventoried without drafting statement prose;
- owner-gated release/submission items remain separate from routine design completion.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` when reproducibility evidence changes a scientific claim;
`argument_spine` when method/reporting evidence changes the central narrative;
`material_local_tradeoff` for a consequential local disclosure/placement choice; and
`deliberate_divergence` only against a grounded template pattern. Credentialed release,
license, or submission authority remains owner-gated by its actual consequence, not by
ceremonial pack approval.

## Invalidation and recheck

Changed code/data bytes, algorithm, parameter, environment, seed, protocol,
availability, license, or checklist version rechecks only dependent `M-*`/`C-*`,
method `PAR-*`, object/visual records, statement inventory, and handoff rows.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not run external production/release actions, certify reproducibility/compliance,
invent artifacts or parameters, confuse planned with available, or draft statements.

## Six-file and hidden projection

00 owns affected scopes/rechecks; 00 route owns release/reporting decisions; 01 owns
method/code/data/reporting artifacts and availability; 02 owns reproducibility claim
ceilings; 03 owns method/algorithm/reporting paragraph and object jobs; 04 compiles
pointers, blockers, prohibitions, and downstream statement handoff. Template dossier
rules may shape reporting presentation but never prove reproducibility.

## Writer-ready consequences

Ready means the requested method/reporting paragraphs can be written from current
artifacts and explicit boundaries, with unresolved release authority isolated. It does
not mean artifacts were released or prose/statements were drafted.
