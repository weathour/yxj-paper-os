# Literature Differentiation

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate when introduction, related-work, contribution contrast, citation placement,
or a research-gap boundary must be designed. Name the affected scopes and whether the
need concerns scientific sourcing, template design, or both.

## Reasoning model

Keep four relations distinct: source truth/relevance, citation function, scientific
support, and rhetorical placement. Group literature by the comparison question it
answers rather than by citation volume alone. A gap is a bounded contrast among what
prior work addresses, what remains unresolved under declared conditions, and what the
current evidence supports; it is not proof of novelty. A publication used both as a
scientific source and a writing template has distinct `M-*` and `TPL-*` identities.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use supplied bibliographic records, source notes, verified identifiers/locators,
related-work matrices, comparator definitions, citation status, counterevidence, and
template provenance. Derive citation-function candidates (background, lineage,
contrast, method, dataset, comparator, limitation), thematic groups, contrast/gap
boundaries, paragraph jobs, and unresolved source needs. Agent external search may
locate eligible candidates when useful, but record query/date/URL/access role and do
not promote snippets or metadata to full reading or scientific authority.

## Sufficiency predicates

- every scientific source role is traceable and separate from claim support;
- citation-function/group/contrast choices have source pointers and limitations;
- gap wording is bounded by reviewed coverage and explicit counterevidence;
- dual-role publications use separate `M-*`/`TPL-*` records and explicit promotion;
- template observations have full-text/derivative provenance and remain design-only;
- paragraph plans say what a citation must do without drafting the sentence.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` when source eligibility changes a claim; `argument_spine`
when literature grouping changes the paper's central contrast; `material_local_tradeoff`
for a locally consequential grouping/placement; and `deliberate_divergence` only from
a grounded template pattern. Routine citation-function placement needs no gate.

## Invalidation and recheck

Changed source truth, version, relevance, comparator identity, access state, dossier
fingerprint, or promotion decision rechecks only linked D07-D12, related-work `PAR-*`,
`TRULE-*`, and handoffs. Template-source staleness cannot stale scientific evidence
unless a distinct `M-*` dependency changed.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not invent citations/BibTeX, treat search snippets as full papers, infer novelty,
use shared-citation frequency as importance, collapse `TPL-*` into `M-*`, or draft
related-work prose.

## Six-file and hidden projection

00 records scope/recheck; 00 route records only triggered positioning decisions; 01
owns scientific source records, research dossier, and separate Template Design
Sources; 02 owns claim/gap wording; 03 owns citation-function paragraph jobs and
semantic `TRULE-*`; 04 points to both 01 authorities. Hidden semantic observations
retain locators, copyright/access limits, fingerprints, and design-only status.

## Writer-ready consequences

Ready means source roles, contrast boundaries, citation functions, paragraph jobs, and
dual-role authority are traceable with no unresolved consequential gate. It neither
proves novelty nor writes literature prose.
