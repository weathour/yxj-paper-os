# Argument, Language, and Visual

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate when reader progression, paragraph organization, terminology, claim verbs,
formula/citation/visual interfaces, caption/legend work, or accessibility can change a
requested surface. Record the exact `SCOPE-*`, sections, and objects affected.

## Reasoning model

Every planned paragraph needs a functional relation between reader state in, payload,
and reader state out. Design all paragraphs at that functional level. Select important
paragraphs qualitatively when commitment density, uncertainty, argument load,
misreading cost, template sensitivity, or formula/citation/visual dependency warrants
finer control. Controlled sentence frames specify proposition slots, clause/relation
order, payload IDs, language contracts, and forbidden realization; they stop before
paste-ready sentences. Visuals are argument objects with evidence, encoding, callout,
caption, legend, accessibility, and non-visual fallback responsibilities.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use route/audience, `C-*`/`M-*` boundaries, section jobs, formulas/algorithms, existing
visuals/data, terminology, limitations, and grounded template observations. Derive
section/paragraph maps, payload boundaries, adaptive frame selection, language
contracts, visual blueprints, and sparse direct `EDGE-*` closure. Generic fallback may
use this lens only through the registered H2/H3 pointer and must remain labeled.

## Sufficiency predicates

- each ready section owns ordered paragraphs with reader-state transitions and adjacency;
- each paragraph has one payload/boundary row and an explicit output promise;
- every selected important paragraph has ordered non-prose `FRM-*` records;
- language contracts bind terminology, verb strength, modality, tense/voice, rhythm,
  and forbidden patterns to grounding IDs;
- visuals/tables bind evidence, panel/order/encoding, caption/legend, callout,
  accessibility, and blocker responsibility;
- direct edges close declared interfaces without inferred graph traversal.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` when a frame/language/visual choice changes claim meaning;
`argument_spine` when reader progression or primary visual order changes the central
answer; `material_local_tradeoff` for a local choice with materially different reader
interpretation; and `deliberate_divergence` only from a grounded template pattern.
Routine paragraph function, terminology repair, frame selection, and accessibility
completion are agent-decided.

## Invalidation and recheck

Changed route, claim/evidence ceiling, formula, source, result, visual bytes, template
fingerprint, or paragraph order rechecks only linked `SEC/PAR/FRM/LANG/VIS/EDGE/TRULE`
records and affected coverage/handoff rows.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not use numeric paragraph-importance scores, require frames for every paragraph,
write cosmetic-fill sentences, let visuals create evidence, infer transitive graph
state, imitate exemplar wording, or draft prose/captions.

## Six-file and hidden projection

00 records scope/readiness/rechecks; 00 route records only triggered spine/tradeoff/
divergence decisions; 01 owns source/result/visual materials; 02 owns scientific
wording; 03 is the authority for paragraph, frame, language, visual, edge, rule, and
budget records; 04 stores seven-surface counts/pointers and handoff. Hidden dossier
observations retain template locators and project compact semantic rules only.

## Writer-ready consequences

Ready means universal paragraph-functional design and every applicable companion
surface are complete, selected important paragraphs have controlled frames, and all
interfaces/prohibitions resolve. It does not mean any manuscript sentence, caption,
title, abstract, or paragraph has been written.
