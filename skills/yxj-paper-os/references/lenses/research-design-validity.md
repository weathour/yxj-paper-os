# Research-Design Validity

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate for empirical, simulation, observational, experimental, formal, or mixed
research surfaces where design assumptions affect what may be claimed. Record the
specific methods/results/discussion scopes and the inference at risk.

## Reasoning model

Separate construct validity (what was measured), internal validity (what alternative
explanations remain), conclusion validity (whether analysis supports the stated
relation), and external validity (where it may transfer). Causal language requires a
causal identification path; prediction, association, simulation response, and formal
property are different claim types. Place a limitation at the first paragraph where
its absence would induce a false interpretation, not only in a terminal limitations
section.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use protocols, assumptions, inclusion/exclusion rules, sampling/allocation, controls,
baselines, metric definitions, calibration, seeds, missingness, failure logs,
formal proofs, and platform boundaries. Derive a threat-to-claim matrix, allowed
causal/non-causal verbs, paragraph-level qualifications, and method/reporting jobs.
Never manufacture a control, randomization, robustness check, or population claim.

## Sufficiency predicates

- each active `C-*` names design basis, scope/conditions, uncertainty, and support;
- material threats and counterevidence map to the exact claim and paragraph affected;
- method paragraphs expose assumptions and design choices needed to interpret results;
- result/discussion paragraphs carry required qualification before generalization;
- causal/non-causal wording and visual/caption statements agree with 02.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` for causal status, target population, estimand, or formal
claim changes. Use `argument_spine` when validity evidence changes the primary result
narrative. Use `material_local_tradeoff` for consequential placement or explanation
choices. `deliberate_divergence` applies only to a grounded template pattern, never to
scientific validity itself. Routine threat placement is agent-decided.

## Invalidation and recheck

Protocol, data-generating process, baseline, metric, seed, assumption, proof, or
platform changes recheck linked `C-*`, method/result `PAR-*`, qualifications,
visual/caption jobs, and D13/D15-D18. Preserve null/adverse findings and unaffected
scopes.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not certify validity, use checklist completion as evidence, upgrade association to
causality, hide adverse findings, or draft methods/results prose.

## Six-file and hidden projection

00 carries affected scopes/rechecks; 00 route carries only route-level validity
choices; 01 inventories protocols/results/artifacts; 02 owns claims, verbs, threats,
and limitations; 03 assigns method/result/limitation paragraph and visual jobs; 04
compiles pointers and prohibitions. Template dossier rules may influence placement or
explanation form only, never validity or support.

## Writer-ready consequences

Ready means every inference-bearing paragraph has the necessary design input,
qualification, and allowed wording pointer, with unresolved scientific choices gated.
It does not mean the study is valid or the prose is written.
