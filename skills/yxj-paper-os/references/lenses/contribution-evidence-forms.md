# Contribution Evidence Forms

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate when a requested scope must distinguish what is contributed, what object is
changed or explained, which comparator/alternative matters, or what evidence can
support the contribution. Name the affected `SCOPE-*`; do not activate merely because
the paper has a contribution section.

## Reasoning model

A contribution is a bounded relation among **referent**, **change/knowledge gain**,
**contrast**, **support**, and **limitation**. Method, system, empirical, resource,
theory, and synthesis contributions realize that relation differently, but none may
borrow support from presentation quality. Separate the contribution claim from the
paragraph jobs that establish context, define the object, expose the mechanism or
result, compare alternatives, and qualify reach. Contribution order is an argument
decision; claim strength remains controlled by 02.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use the project brief, route, `M-*` artifacts/evidence, `C-*` claims, comparator facts,
formal assumptions, result locks, and stated limitations. Safely derive candidate
contribution forms, section/paragraph responsibilities, claim-to-material pointers,
and rejected overclaim boundaries. Never infer novelty, comparator fidelity, or
completed evidence from a label such as “method” or “framework.”

## Sufficiency predicates

- each contribution option names referent, change, contrast, support, and limitation;
- 02 contains the active/deferred/rejected claim ceiling and wording boundary;
- 03 assigns section/paragraph jobs, reader-state transitions, payload IDs, output
  promises, and forbidden overclaim;
- competing contribution orders are reconciled or a gate is resolved;
- unsupported alternatives remain deferred/rejected rather than rhetorically repaired.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` when the scientific referent or claim ceiling changes.
Use `argument_spine` when contribution/result ordering changes the paper's primary
answer. Use `material_local_tradeoff` when a local framing choice materially changes
reader interpretation. Use `deliberate_divergence` only against a grounded template
pattern. Routine contribution-form labeling and paragraph placement need no gate.

## Invalidation and recheck

A changed claim, evidence anchor, comparator status, route, result lock, or limitation
rechecks only linked D10-D17 records, `PAR-*` payloads, contribution-order decisions,
and affected handoff rows. Do not stale unrelated scopes.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not turn a contribution taxonomy into a score, infer novelty, let template rules
raise D11 support, create ceremonial pack approval, or draft contribution prose.

## Six-file and hidden projection

- 00: affected scope, requirements, dependency recheck;
- 00 route: only triggered contribution/order decisions;
- 01: eligible `M-*` inputs and explicit absences;
- 02: contribution options, `C-*` support/wording ceiling, limitations;
- 03: section/paragraph/payload/frame/language/edge responsibilities;
- 04: compact coverage, blockers, prohibitions, handoff pointers.

Template observations, when applicable, live in the hidden semantic dossier and
project only as design-only `TRULE-*`; they never become contribution evidence.

## Writer-ready consequences

A scope is structurally ready only when its contribution referent and evidence ceiling
are stable, paragraph responsibilities close the contribution relation, triggered
four-gate choices resolve, and 04 points to those records. No manuscript sentence is
produced.
