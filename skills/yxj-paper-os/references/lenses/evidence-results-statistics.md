# Evidence, Results, and Statistics

## Activation and affected scopes

Activate when project results, metrics, uncertainty, failures, figures, tables, or
statistical displays support a requested scope. This lens owns project-result design;
template-corpus statistics remain in `venue-template` and cannot support D06/D11.

## Reasoning model

Design result communication as a sequence of functions: analysis context, primary
finding, comparison, robustness/sensitivity, failure/null/adverse result, and bounded
interpretation. A number is not self-interpreting: retain unit, denominator,
aggregation level, uncertainty, calibration/selection context, and missingness.
Figures and tables carry evidence only through their underlying `M-*` records;
captions explain the display and qualification but create no new result.

## Repository inputs and safe derivations

Use locked result files, tables, plots, run manifests, metric definitions, statistical
outputs, uncertainty intervals, failures/nulls, calibration records, and explicit
non-comparability. Derive result paragraph jobs, display order, panel/encoding choices,
caption/legend responsibilities, callout relations, and required limitations. Do not
pool incompatible strata or invent effect size, significance, uncertainty, or outcome.

## Sufficiency predicates

- every result claim resolves to eligible `M-*` evidence and a D11 warrant;
- metric/unit/denominator/aggregation/missingness and uncertainty are explicit;
- failures, nulls, adverse findings, and incomparable cases have dispositions;
- each result paragraph has a function, payload boundary, and qualification;
- each visual/table has evidence IDs, story role, encoding/order, caption/legend,
  callout, accessibility, and blocker state.

## Four-gate mapping

Use `scientific_commitment` for result interpretation or evidence ceiling changes;
`argument_spine` for the primary result narrative/order; `material_local_tradeoff`
when a display or local explanation materially changes interpretation; and
`deliberate_divergence` only from a grounded template pattern. Routine display repair
or null-result placement is agent-led.

## Invalidation and recheck

Changed result bytes, metric definitions, calibration, strata, uncertainty, failures,
or visual source recheck only linked `C-*`, result `PAR-*`, `VIS-*`, `EDGE-*`, captions,
and handoff rows. Retain adverse evidence rather than deleting it during recompile.

## Failure modes and non-goals

Do not create an arbitrary composite, suppress null/adverse findings, treat template
frequencies as project results, equate visual salience with evidence strength, or draft
result/caption prose.

## Six-file and hidden projection

00 declares affected result scopes/rechecks; 01 owns result/evidence/visual artifacts;
02 owns support and wording; 03 owns result paragraph, language, visual, caption,
accessibility, and traceability records; 04 compiles coverage and blockers. Hidden
template analysis may shape presentation only and remains design-only.

## Writer-ready consequences

A result scope is ready only when claims, statistics, failures, paragraph functions,
and visual/caption responsibilities agree and all linked artifacts are current. This
is a controlled writing handoff, not a finding-quality judgment.
