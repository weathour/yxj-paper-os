When platoon coordination loses a trusted authority signal, the paper treats reconfiguration as a bounded control problem rather than as a unbounded safety promise. The candidate mechanism first isolates the degraded authority path, then shifts the affected vehicle group toward a constrained coordination mode whose scope is limited by the available evidence and assumptions. Figure 1 is therefore used as an explanatory map of the authority-loss response, not as empirical proof of performance.
